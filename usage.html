Usage & Safety Tips

Best practices for safe and efficient generator operation

Using Generators Safely

  • Location Matters: Always operate generators outdoors in well-ventilated areas to prevent carbon monoxide poisoning. Never run them indoors, in garages, or in enclosed spaces.
  • Protect from Elements: Keep the unit dry and protected from rain, snow, or water. Use a canopy or shelter, but ensure adequate ventilation to prevent overheating.
  • Proper Wiring: Use properly rated extension cords and avoid overloading the generator. Check cord integrity regularly and never run cords through water.
  • Safe Refueling: Turn off the generator and let it cool completely before refueling to prevent fires. Store fuel in approved containers away from the generator.
  • Read the Manual: Carefully review your generator's owner's manual and understand all specifications, safety features, and maintenance requirements before operating.
  • Regular Inspection: Check for fuel leaks, loose connections, and damaged components before each use. Address any issues immediately.
  • Grounding: Properly ground your generator to prevent electrical hazards. Follow manufacturer guidelines for grounding procedures.
